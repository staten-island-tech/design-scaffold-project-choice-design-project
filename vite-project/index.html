New York City

Attractions

The Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty represents a Roman Goddess gifted from France to U.S.A to symbolize America's liberty. Tourists arrive and depart Liberty Island and Ellis Island, located in New York Harbor, via ferries operated by Statue City Cruises. Visitors can learn about the history of Liberty Island and explore museums and exhibits on site.

Times Square

Times Square is a major commercial intersection, tourist destination, entertainment center, and neighborhood in midtown Manhattan. With its neon lights and billboards, Times Square is New York's most famous landmark and is the liveliest area in the city, located at the intersection of Broadway and 7th Avenue. You will find bars, restaurants, theatres, museums, and cartoon characters you can take pictures with. No wonder why it attracts over 330,000 people each day!

The Brooklyn Bridge

The Brooklyn Bridge, built in 1883, was the longest suspension bridge in the world by the time of completion. It is 5,989 feet long or 1825 meters from one end to the other. The Brooklyn Bridge is a suspension bridge that connects Brooklyn to Manhattan. Tourists often walk, run, or bike across the bridge while enoying a stunning view of the Manhattan skyline. They take in the views of both Brooklyn, Manhattan, and the Statue of Liberty while there is still light out and then watch as all the sights come to life with electric wonder.
