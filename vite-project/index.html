New York City

Attractions

The Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty represents a Roman Goddess gifted from France to U.S.A to symbolize America's liberty. Tourists arrive and depart Liberty Island and Ellis Island, located in New York Harbor, via ferries operated by Statue City Cruises. Visitors can learn about the history of Liberty Island and explore museums and exhibits on site.

Times Square

Times Square is a major commercial intersection, tourist destination, entertainment center, and neighborhood in midtown Manhattan. With its neon lights and billboards, Times Square is New York's most famous landmark and is the liveliest area in the city, located at the intersection of Broadway and 7th Avenue. You will find bars, restaurants, theatres, museums, and cartoon characters you can take pictures with. No wonder why it attracts over 330,000 people each day!

The Brooklyn Bridge

The Brooklyn Bridge, built in 1883, was the longest suspension bridge in the world by the time of completion. It is 5,989 feet long or 1825 meters from one end to the other. The Brooklyn Bridge is a suspension bridge that connects Brooklyn to Manhattan. Tourists often walk, run, or bike across the bridge while enoying a stunning view of the Manhattan skyline. They take in the views of both Brooklyn, Manhattan, and the Statue of Liberty while there is still light out and then watch as all the sights come to life with electric wonder.

Food

Breakfast
It is often said that breakfast is the most important meal of the day. This is especially true for New-Yorkers and tourists. An average NYC breakfast includes hot/iced coffee , bagels, muffins, bacon and egg sandwiches. You can find these items at your local grocery/convenience store for under $5. A refreshing/cheap way to start off your day!
Lunch
New York City includes one of the most diverse variety of cultures that can be found in the resturants. These include American, A sian, European, and many more resturants that all contain delicious food. Perhaps the most popular lunch is pizza and Chinese food. You can usually get a full lunch for under $15. Make sure to save some room for dinner!
Dinner
Dinner is the last meal of the day, so make sure to stuff up! Many New Yorkers/tourists tend to eat more fancy/expensive meals for dinner. These meals may include steak, seafood, sushi, tacos, salads, ramen, hotpot, barbecue, and many more! You don't have to choose from this list, there are over 23,000 resturants, all with unique dishes, in NYC!

Teams

New York currently has 11 major league sport teams, but below are the four most popular teams/stadiums in New York City.

Brooklyn Nets

The Brooklyn Nets was originally founded in 1967, known as the New Jersey Nets, however, the Nets currently play in Brooklyn in Barclays Center located in Atlantic Avenue. They currently have a few superstars like Kevin Durant and Kyrie Irving. The Barclays center can hold up to 19,000 people at once, with 100+ luxury suites, bars, lounges, and resturants.

New York Knicks

The New York Knicks was orginally founded in 1946, and has a rich history along with 2 championships. Like the Brooklyn Nets, the Knicks are also an NBA team in NYC. Madison Square Garden is known as "The World's Most Famous Arena". It hosts sporting games, concerts, and many other events!

New York Yankees

The New York Yankees are an American professional baseball team (MLB) based in the New York City borough of the Bronx. They are one of the most successful sport franchises with 27 World Series titles. Yankee Stadium attracts a regular 25,000 people for each game!

New York Rangers

The New York Rangers are a professional NHL ice hockey team based in New York City. Like the Knicks, they play in Madison Square Garden but has won the Stanley Cup, the NHL's championship trophy four times!

Boroughs

Bronx

The Bronx is the birthplace of rap/hip hop music and home to one of the country's most known professional baseball teams, the New York Yankees. While your in the area, head on over to the Bronx Zoo!

Brooklyn

Brooklyn is home of iconic New York attractions like Coney Island, the Brooklyn Bridge and Prospect Park, plus some of the best New York pizza. In addition, Brooklyn has its own NBA team, The Brooklyn Nets!

Manhattan

Manhattan is considered one of the world's foremost commercial, financial, and cultural centres. Its iconic sites include skyscrapers such as the Empire State Building, Times Square, Broadway, Madison Square Garden, and the beautiful Central Park.

Queens

Queens is home to two of the three major NYC area airports, JFK International and LaGuardia. Attractions include Flushing Meadows Park—home to the New York Mets baseball team at Citi Field.

Staten Island

Staten Island is well-known for its green park spaces, museums and historical buildings. It is often considered the “borough of parks” because of its numerous natural spaces. Notable parks include Clove Lakes, High Rock Park, and Lemon Creek Park.
